## Summary
This article is a beginner's guide to Snowflake aimed at data engineering interview preparation. It covers Snowflake's three-layer architecture, the separation of compute and storage, virtual warehouses, Time Travel, zero-copy cloning, micro-partitions, and query pruning. The guide also compares Snowflake to competing platforms such as Redshift, BigQuery, Databricks, and Azure Synapse. It is structured as a tutorial with worked examples and interview-style practice problems, targeting freshers entering the data engineering field.

## Key claims
- Snowflake is a cloud-native data warehouse that stores petabytes and runs analytics in seconds.
- Snowflake separates compute and storage independently, unlike legacy MPP warehouses.
- Snowflake offers features such as Time Travel, zero-copy cloning, and secure data sharing that legacy warehouses cannot match.
- Snowflake's architecture is fundamentally different from traditional databases like Postgres or MySQL.
- The article compares Snowflake to Redshift, BigQuery, Databricks, and Azure Synapse.
- Micro-partitions and query pruning are key Snowflake performance optimization concepts covered in the guide.
