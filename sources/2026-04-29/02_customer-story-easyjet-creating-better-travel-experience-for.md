## Summary
easyJet, one of Europe's leading low-cost airlines, is modernising its decade-old revenue management stack by migrating to the Databricks Data Intelligence Platform. The airline is replacing a legacy desktop RMS and a sprawling SQL Server estate with a unified, governed hub built on Lakebase, Delta Lake, Unity Catalog, Lakeflow, and Databricks Apps. Key outcomes include reducing app delivery time from 6–9 months to approximately 4 months and consolidating over 100 Git repositories into just 2. The new architecture unifies operational and analytical data, enabling trading teams to analyse demand and act on pricing strategies in a single governed workspace. Dennis Michon, Head of Data Product at easyJet, credits the platform with delivering a faster, simpler, and more reliable revenue management solution.

## Key claims
- easyJet reduced revenue management app delivery time from 6–9 months to approximately 4 months using the Databricks Data Intelligence Platform.
- More than 100 Git repositories were consolidated into just 2, streamlining pipelines, CI/CD, and maintenance.
- Lakebase captures live booking and pricing activity while Delta Lake powers analytics from the same lakehouse.
- Unity Catalog governs access end-to-end across easyJet's new data platform.
- Lakeflow Jobs Declarative Pipelines automate fare updates, reports, and checks with less manual oversight.
- Databricks Apps provide trading teams a single governed workspace to analyse demand, compare fares, and act on pricing strategies.
- easyJet is replacing a legacy desktop RMS and a sprawling SQL Server estate with a unified commercial decision-making hub.
