## Summary
DataHacks 2026 was a 36-hour MLH-certified hackathon hosted by UC San Diego's Data Science Student Society (DS3), drawing over 400 students. Databricks participated through its University Alliances program, sponsoring a dedicated challenge track and providing mentorship, workshops, and booth presence. Workshop lead Anjana Sriram argues that early hands-on exposure to a platform creates lasting professional bias toward that tool, making student hackathons a high-leverage investment in long-term platform adoption. Thirteen teams submitted Databricks Challenge projects, and Databricks mentors spent 12 hours guiding students through architecture decisions and pipeline debugging. The event showcased tools including Lakeflow, MLflow, and Genie as part of the Databricks Free Edition experience.

## Key claims
- Over 400 students participated in DataHacks 2026 at UC San Diego, spending 36 hours building real projects.
- Databricks sponsored a dedicated challenge track at DataHacks through its University Alliances program.
- 13 teams submitted projects for the Databricks Challenge, which were evaluated by Databricks judges.
- Anjana Sriram spent 12 hours mentoring student teams and spoke with over 100 students during the event.
- The University Alliances program aims to create long-term platform adoption by giving students early hands-on experience with Databricks Free Edition.
- Students used Lakeflow, MLflow, and Genie as part of their Databricks projects at the hackathon.
- The Databricks booth at DataHacks was described as busier than the adjacent AWS booth.
