## Summary
Dr. Kashyap Thimmaraju and Giuseppe Canale from Silicon Psyche have built Posture Sequence Analysis (PSA), a behavioral health monitor for LLMs and AI agents. PSA operationalizes their Cybersecurity Psychology Framework (CPF3), which posits that LLMs inherit human-like psychological vulnerabilities because they were trained on human-generated data. The tool uses five classifiers to systematically measure behavioral states including adversarial stress, sycophancy, hallucination risk, persuasion technique detection, and action-risk in agentic systems. PSA grew out of the founders' earlier work jailbreaking frontier models like Claude Opus, after which they pivoted from exploitation to behavioral analysis. It currently integrates with LangFuse and ElevenLabs observability platforms via API.

## Key claims
- PSA uses five classifiers (C0–C5) to deterministically measure the behavioral state of LLMs and AI agents.
- The Cybersecurity Psychology Framework (CPF3) theorizes that LLMs inherit human-like psychological vulnerabilities from training on human-generated data.
- The authors developed a methodology to jailbreak Claude Opus 4.6 and other frontier models, prompting Anthropic to delete conversations and block their approach.
- PSA can detect adversarial pressure, sycophantic drift, hallucination risk, and persuasion techniques in real time.
- PSA integrates with LangFuse and ElevenLabs via API and can generate plugins for similar observability platforms.
- The system includes graph topology, Bayesian alignment detection, cross-agent contagion metrics, and hidden-state temporal prediction components.
- The authors published a paper on Silicon Psyche and anthropomorphic vulnerabilities in LLMs on arXiv (2601.00867).
