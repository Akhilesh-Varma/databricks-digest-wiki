## Summary
Glia is an AI architecture for networked systems design that leverages large language models within a human-inspired multi-agent workflow. Each agent specializes in reasoning, experimentation, and analysis, collaborating through an evaluation framework that grounds abstract reasoning in empirical feedback. Unlike prior ML-for-systems approaches that optimize black-box policies, Glia generates interpretable designs and exposes its reasoning process. When applied to a distributed GPU cluster for LLM inference, Glia produces novel algorithms for request routing, scheduling, and auto-scaling that match human-expert performance in significantly less time. The results suggest that combining reasoning LLMs with structured experimentation enables creative and understandable solutions to complex systems problems.
