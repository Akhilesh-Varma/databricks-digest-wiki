## Summary
The article examines Delta Live Tables (DLT), Databricks' declarative pipeline framework, through the lens of building a Medallion architecture pipeline on the Databricks Free Edition. It highlights the power of DLT for simplifying data pipeline development while also exposing real-world constraints and trade-offs encountered in production scenarios. The author explores how declarative frameworks differ from imperative approaches and where architectural limitations emerge. The piece is aimed at data engineers evaluating DLT for serious workloads.

## Key claims
- Delta Live Tables is a declarative pipeline framework offered by Databricks.
- Building a Medallion pipeline on Databricks Free Edition reveals practical constraints of DLT.
- Declarative frameworks like DLT involve notable production trade-offs compared to imperative approaches.
- DLT imposes architectural constraints that become apparent at scale or in complex pipeline scenarios.
- The Medallion architecture (bronze, silver, gold layers) was used as the test pattern for evaluating DLT.
