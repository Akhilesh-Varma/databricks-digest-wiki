## Summary
Databricks has expanded serverless compute to power Notebooks, Lakeflow Jobs, and Spark Declarative Pipelines, eliminating the need for manual cluster management. Over the past year, serverless workloads have become 80% faster and up to 70% more cost-efficient without user intervention. The platform has applied 25 DBR upgrades across 4.5 billion workloads with a 99.998% success rate. New performance modes offer either faster execution or significant cost savings for batch workloads, while built-in cost governance tools provide unified billing and budget policies.

## Key claims
- Serverless compute on Databricks now powers Notebooks, Lakeflow Jobs, and Spark Declarative Pipelines.
- Serverless workloads have become 80% faster and up to 70% more cost-efficient over the last year without any user changes.
- Automatic scaling and failover have delivered 89% more successful runs compared to classic clusters.
- Databricks has applied 25 DBR upgrades across 4.5 billion workloads with a 99.998% success rate.
- A Performance-optimized mode starts in seconds and typically runs about 2x faster than standard configurations.
- Standard mode can cut job costs by up to 70% for batch workloads.
- Built-in cost governance includes unified billing, budget policies, and intelligent timeouts for serverless spend.
