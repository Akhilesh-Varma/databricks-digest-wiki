## Summary
The article introduces the Medallion Architecture as a solution to disorganized data pipelines in modern data engineering. It explains how this architecture organizes data into layered zones, typically Bronze, Silver, and Gold, to progressively refine raw data into analytics-ready datasets. The approach is associated with the Data Lakehouse paradigm, which combines cheap storage with structured data management. The article positions Medallion Architecture as a best practice for teams looking to bring order and reliability to their data workflows.

## Key claims
- The Medallion Architecture is presented as a solution to messy, disorganized data pipelines.
- The architecture organizes data into progressive layers, commonly Bronze, Silver, and Gold.
- Each layer refines data further, moving from raw ingestion to analytics-ready datasets.
- The Medallion Architecture is closely associated with the Data Lakehouse paradigm.
- The Data Lakehouse combines cheap storage with structured, reliable data management.
