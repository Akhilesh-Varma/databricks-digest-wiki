## Summary
An analysis of 6,736 active Data Engineer job postings on InterviewStack.io as of May 2026 reveals a significant but uneven AI-driven shift in the role. About 39.5% of postings mention some form of AI, while 17.4% explicitly require new-wave generative AI skills such as LLMs, RAG, AI agents, and vector databases. Roles with new-wave AI requirements command a median salary premium of roughly $18,965 over non-AI roles. Healthcare leads all industries in explicit AI adoption at 27.9%, and senior roles show notably higher AI adoption rates than entry-level positions. A separate 'ambient' shift is also underway, with 72% of data practitioners reportedly using AI coding tools daily, a figure more than four times the explicit posting rate.

## Key claims
- 39.5% of 6,736 active Data Engineer postings mention some form of AI, including traditional ML.
- 17.4% of postings explicitly require new-wave generative AI skills such as LLMs, RAG, AI agents, and vector databases.
- US-based roles with new-wave AI requirements carry a median salary premium of $18,965 compared to non-AI roles ($136,520 vs $117,555).
- Machine Learning leads all AI skills mentioned in postings at 30.6%, followed by LLMs at 6.7% and AI Agents at 6.6%.
- Healthcare leads all industries in explicit AI adoption at 27.9%, ahead of technology at 22.4% and software at 21.5%.
- Senior roles show 18.3% AI adoption versus 12.3% for entry-level roles, indicating AI infrastructure is predominantly senior-level work.
- 72% of data practitioners use AI coding tools daily according to dbt Labs 2026 survey, more than four times the explicit posting rate.
