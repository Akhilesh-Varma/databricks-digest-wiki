## Summary
A 46-year-old Amazon warehouse worker collapsed and died on April 6 at the PDX9 fulfillment center in Troutdale, Oregon. Multiple employees allege that for over an hour after the collapse, managers instructed workers to continue loading trucks and picking items while the body remained on the warehouse floor. A CPR-trained co-worker was reportedly turned away by a manager when offering to help. Amazon disputed this account, stating that three CPR-certified team members responded immediately, the area was cordoned off, and operations were closed shortly after EMS arrived. Discrepancies remain between employee accounts and Amazon's official statement regarding how the situation was handled.

## Key claims
- A 46-year-old Amazon worker collapsed and died on the floor of the PDX9 fulfillment center in Troutdale, Oregon on April 6.
- Multiple employees allege managers told workers to keep loading trucks and picking items for more than an hour while the body lay nearby.
- A CPR-trained co-worker who offered to assist was reportedly told by a manager to turn around and get back to work.
- Amazon spokesperson Sam Stephenson said the worker died from a pre-existing medical issue and that three CPR-certified team members responded and deployed an automated defibrillator.
- Amazon told Moneywise it closed operations shortly after EMS arrived and sent workers home with pay, and cancelled the night shift.
- At least one employee reported not receiving full pay for the shift a week after the incident.
- Workers received a text that evening instructing them to report to work as usual the next day.
