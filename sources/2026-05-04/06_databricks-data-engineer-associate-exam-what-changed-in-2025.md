## Summary
Milka Kutseva, a Data Engineer at DataArt, shares her experience earning the Databricks Data Engineer Associate certification and breaks down what changed in the July 2025 exam update. The certification tests practical skills including building data pipelines, working with Delta tables, Spark SQL, PySpark, and workflow orchestration across 45 multiple-choice questions in 90 minutes. The July 2025 update shifted focus toward the Databricks Data Intelligence Platform, added more scenario-based questions, and expanded coverage of Delta Live Tables, Unity Catalog, Delta Sharing, and Serverless compute. She recommends structured Udemy courses for foundational knowledge followed by intensive practice with exam-style question sets. Older study materials may no longer align with the updated exam format, making it important to use current resources.

## Key claims
- The Databricks Data Engineer Associate exam consists of 45 multiple-choice questions to be completed in 90 minutes in an online proctored format.
- In July 2025, Databricks updated the exam to place stronger emphasis on the Databricks Data Intelligence Platform beyond the traditional Lakehouse model.
- The 2025 update expanded exam coverage to include Delta Live Tables, Unity Catalog, data governance, Delta Sharing, Serverless compute, and performance optimization.
- The updated exam features more scenario-based questions focused on decision-making and problem-solving rather than abstract theory.
- Older learning materials and practice tests may no longer align with the July 2025 exam format.
- Milka Kutseva recommends combining structured Udemy courses with dedicated practice exam sets as the most effective preparation strategy.
