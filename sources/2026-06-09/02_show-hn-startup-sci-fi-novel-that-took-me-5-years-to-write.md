## Summary
A startup founder with 15 years of experience spent five years writing a sci-fi thriller novel called 'Blockchained,' inspired by Stephen King's advice to write about a domain you know intimately. The manuscript evolved from a 1,000-word exercise into a 125,000-word draft, then was refined to 88,000 words through multiple rewrites, literary study, and professional editorial feedback. After 100 failed pitches to literary agents, the author chose to self-publish. The novel follows a struggling startup founder who encounters a mysterious Chinese government-linked investor in Hong Kong. The author emphasizes the book was written by hand with no AI authorship, only minor AI-assisted tools for research and grammar.

## Key claims
- The author spent 5 years writing and refining the novel 'Blockchained,' starting from a 1,000-word exercise inspired by Stephen King's 'On Writing.'
- The manuscript was rewritten multiple times, ultimately distilled from 125,000 words to 88,000 words with help from professional editor and author Rob Hart.
- After 100 personalized pitches to literary agents, the author received zero offers of representation.
- The novel is described as a near-future startup sci-fi thriller set partly in Hong Kong involving a Chinese government-linked investor.
- The author states the novel was 99.9% human-written, with AI used only for research, spellcheck, grammar, and occasional phrasing checks.
- The author identified startup founders as too small a TAM for traditional literary agents to be interested in representing the book.
