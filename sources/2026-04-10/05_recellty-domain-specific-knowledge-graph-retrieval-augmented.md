## Summary
ReCellTy is a domain-specific system that combines a knowledge graph with large language models to improve automated single-cell type annotation. The knowledge graph contains 18,850 biological information nodes—including cell types, gene markers, and related entities—connected by 48,944 edges. A multi-task reasoning workflow is designed to optimize the annotation process by guiding LLMs to retrieve entities associated with differential genes. Compared to general-purpose LLMs, ReCellTy improves human evaluation scores by up to 0.21 and semantic similarity by 6.1 across multiple tissue types. The approach also narrows the performance gap between large and small LLMs in cell type annotation tasks.

## Key claims
- ReCellTy constructs a knowledge graph with 18,850 biological nodes and 48,944 edges covering cell types, gene markers, and related entities.
- The system uses the knowledge graph to guide LLMs in retrieving entities associated with differential genes for cell reconstruction.
- A multi-task reasoning workflow is designed to optimize the cell type annotation process.
- ReCellTy improves human evaluation scores by up to 0.21 over general-purpose LLMs across multiple tissue types.
- Semantic similarity is improved by 6.1 percentage points compared to general-purpose LLMs.
- The method narrows the performance gap between large and small LLMs in cell type annotation.
- The approach more closely aligns with the cognitive logic of manual annotation compared to general-purpose LLMs.
