## Summary
This position paper argues that scaling laws for large language models face two critical bottlenecks: the depletion of high-quality public training data and the prohibitive computational costs monopolized by large tech companies. The authors propose leveraging massive distributed edge devices as a solution to break through these barriers. They review recent advances in distributed federated learning that make this paradigm viable, suggesting that collaborative training across edge devices could democratize AI development. The paper contends that this shift could allow anyone with small edge devices to participate in training large language models, fostering a more inclusive AI community.

## Key claims
- LLM scaling laws face two critical bottlenecks: depletion of high-quality public data and prohibitive computational costs monopolized by tech giants.
- Massive distributed edge devices contain vast untapped data and computational resources that could address these bottlenecks.
- Recent advances in distributed federated learning make edge-based collaborative LLM training viable.
- Distributed training on edge devices could democratize AI development by enabling participation with small consumer hardware.
- The proposed paradigm shift could foster a more inclusive AI community beyond large technology organizations.
