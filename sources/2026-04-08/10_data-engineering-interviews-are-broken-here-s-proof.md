## Summary
The article argues that data engineering interviews are fundamentally broken, citing misaligned assessment methods such as LeetCode-style algorithmic challenges for pipeline-focused roles. It highlights the burden of lengthy take-home assignments and notes that AI tools have effectively replaced many of the skills traditionally tested in these interviews. The author contends that the current interview process fails both candidates and hiring organizations.

## Key claims
- Data engineering interviews rely heavily on LeetCode-style algorithmic questions that are misaligned with actual pipeline work.
- Take-home assignments in DE interviews can span up to 10 hours, placing an unreasonable burden on candidates.
- AI tools have replaced many of the skills that data engineering interviews traditionally assess.
- The current DE interview process is failing both candidates and employers.
