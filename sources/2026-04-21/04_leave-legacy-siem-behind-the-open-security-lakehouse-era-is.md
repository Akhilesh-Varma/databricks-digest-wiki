## Summary
Databricks CEO Ali Ghodsi and Ben Horowitz, Co-founder and General Partner at Andreessen Horowitz, recently delivered a keynote speech at RSA 2026, discussing the need to move beyond legacy SIEMs to an open security lakehouse. They highlighted the limitations of manual SOCs in keeping up with zero-day exploits, which now take only 1.3 days to execute. The keynote also covered the benefits of using Lakewatch AI agents and building on Unity Catalog and OCSF for an open security lakehouse. The speakers emphasized that the physics of cyber defense have changed, requiring a new approach to security. The keynote is available for replay, and viewers are encouraged to share their thoughts on SIEM vs. lakehouse.

## Key claims
- Zero-day exploits now take only 1.3 days to execute, outpacing manual SOCs.
- Legacy SIEMs impose a data tax, requiring users to discard valuable telemetry.
- An open security lakehouse powered by OCSF and Unity Catalog can help fight AI threat swarms.
- Lakewatch AI agents can be used to analyze multimodal data, including video, audio, and chat.
- The physics of cyber defense have changed, necessitating a shift from legacy SIEMs to an open security lakehouse.
