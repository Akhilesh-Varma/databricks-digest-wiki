## Summary
Columnar databases like ClickHouse and Snowflake are changing the data landscape by storing data column by column, making them ideal for analytical queries. This approach allows for better compression and faster queries. ClickHouse and Snowflake are two heavyweights in the columnar database world, leveraging this architecture to achieve high performance for analytical workloads. They primarily use SQL for data manipulation and queries. Understanding the basics of columnar databases and SQL can help appreciate these tools.

## Key claims
- Columnar databases store data column by column, making them suitable for analytical queries.
- ClickHouse and Snowflake are examples of databases that use columnar architecture for high performance.
- Columnar databases offer better compression and faster queries compared to traditional row-based databases.
- SQL is the primary language used for data manipulation and queries in ClickHouse and Snowflake.
- Columnar databases reduce I/O operations, which are a major bottleneck in data analytics.
