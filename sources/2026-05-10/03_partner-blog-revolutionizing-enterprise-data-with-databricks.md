## Summary
This article describes how Databricks Agent Bricks, a multi-agent AI framework, is being applied in two enterprise industries: financial services and healthcare. In the financial use case, a multi-agent architecture combines a Knowledge Assistant for unstructured data and a Genie space for structured Delta table data, orchestrated by a Supervisor Agent to enable proactive risk management. In the healthcare use case, a cross-domain conversational agent breaks down siloed Gold layer data domains to allow natural language querying across Sales, Finance, and HR data. Both implementations aim to democratize data access for non-technical users and drive faster, smarter decision-making. The solutions leverage Databricks Volumes, Delta tables, and enriched business metadata to ground AI responses in domain-specific context.

## Key claims
- Databricks Agent Bricks uses a multi-agent architecture combining a Knowledge Assistant and a Genie space orchestrated by a Supervisor Agent.
- Financial institutions can use Agent Bricks to proactively monitor liquidity coverage ratios, operational risk events, and cybersecurity vulnerabilities.
- Business metadata enrichment of Delta tables with comments, tags, thresholds, and risk categories enables AI to converse in true business language.
- A healthcare organization used Agent Bricks to enable cross-domain natural language querying across siloed Gold layer data domains including Sales, Finance, and HR.
- Agent Bricks includes an in-built LLM that provides external intelligence such as industry standards, competitor actions, and remediation strategies.
- The Supervisor Agent is deployed via a UI interface and is given guardrails to prevent out-of-context questions.
- The healthcare solution aimed to democratize governed data access for non-technical business users without requiring SQL knowledge.
