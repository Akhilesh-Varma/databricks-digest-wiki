## Summary
An Amazon warehouse employee died on the job at the PDX9 fulfillment center in Troutdale, Oregon. The worker collapsed on the floor and lay dead while other employees continued working around him. Workers on Reddit alleged that recently installed soundproof curtains had restricted airflow and raised temperatures, potentially contributing to the death, though Amazon stated that Oregon OSHA determined the incident to be non-work related. The PDX9 facility has a documented history of high injury rates, and Amazon's fulfillment centers broadly report serious injuries at more than twice the warehouse industry average. The U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York has an ongoing investigation into workplace safety at Amazon warehouses.

## Key claims
- An Amazon employee at the PDX9 warehouse in Troutdale, Oregon collapsed and died on the job.
- Workers on Reddit alleged that newly installed soundproof curtains restricted airflow and made the building unusually hot.
- Oregon OSHA determined the incident to be non-work related, according to Amazon.
- A 2018 investigation found that 26% of PDX9 employees had sustained injuries.
- Amazon fulfillment centers report serious injuries at more than twice the warehouse industry average based on 2024 OSHA data.
- Federal investigators have alleged that Amazon manipulated data and failed to properly document workplace injuries.
- The U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York is conducting an ongoing investigation into workplace safety at Amazon warehouses.
