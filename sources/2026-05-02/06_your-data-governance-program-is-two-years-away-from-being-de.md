## Summary
The article argues that data governance programs are typically funded on optimism in their first year but must demonstrate measurable value by year two or risk being defunded. It warns that activity-based metrics such as policy counts are insufficient to justify continued investment. The piece implies that governance teams need to shift toward outcome-driven metrics to survive budget scrutiny. The full argument is published on the Towards Data Engineering publication on Medium.

## Key claims
- Year one of a data governance program is funded on hope rather than demonstrated results.
- Year two funding depends on metrics, not just activity.
- Counting policies or similar activity metrics is insufficient to justify a governance program's budget.
- Data governance programs that cannot show business outcomes risk being defunded within two years.
