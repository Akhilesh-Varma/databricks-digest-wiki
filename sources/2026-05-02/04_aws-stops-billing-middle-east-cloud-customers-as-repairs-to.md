## Summary
Iranian drone strikes in early 2026 damaged three Amazon Web Services data centers in the United Arab Emirates and Bahrain, disrupting cloud services in the ME-CENTRAL-1 and ME-SOUTH-1 regions. AWS has suspended billing for affected customers while repairs continue, a process expected to take several more months, meaning total recovery could span nearly half a year. Amazon initially waived all March 2026 usage charges at an estimated cost of $150 million. AWS has urged customers to migrate workloads to unaffected regions and restore data from remote backups. Some customers, like Dubai-based super app Careem, successfully migrated overnight to resume operations.

## Key claims
- Iranian drone strikes targeted three AWS data centers in the UAE and Bahrain in early March 2026.
- AWS suspended billing for customers in the ME-CENTRAL-1 and ME-SOUTH-1 regions while repairs are ongoing.
- Full recovery from the cloud disruption is expected to take nearly half a year in total.
- Amazon waived all usage-related charges for March 2026 at an estimated cost of $150 million.
- AWS strongly recommended that affected customers migrate resources to other cloud regions.
- Dubai-based super app Careem completed an overnight migration to other data center servers and restored service quickly.
