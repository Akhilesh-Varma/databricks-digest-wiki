## Summary
The article examines the predictable failure modes of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems that rely on vector search and embeddings for enterprise document intelligence. It argues that while embedding-based retrieval handles synonyms and paraphrase well, it silently fails on negation, exact identifiers, and company-specific acronyms. The piece is framed as the first installment of an 'Enterprise Document Intelligence' series on Towards Data Science. It suggests that practitioners need to understand these limitations and consider alternative or complementary retrieval strategies.

## Key claims
- Vector search handles synonyms and paraphrase effectively but fails silently on negation.
- Embedding-based retrieval struggles with exact identifiers such as product codes or IDs.
- Company-specific acronyms represent a predictable failure mode for RAG retrieval systems.
- The failure modes of RAG retrieval are predictable and not random or mysterious.
- Alternative retrieval methods should be considered when embeddings are insufficient for specific query types.
