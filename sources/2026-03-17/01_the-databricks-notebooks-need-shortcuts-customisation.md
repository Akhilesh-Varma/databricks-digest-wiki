## Summary
A long-time Databricks user and former AmpLab contributor expresses frustration with the lack of keyboard shortcut customization in Databricks notebooks. The author, who has decades of experience with JetBrains IDEs and Visual Studio Code, finds the web-only editor limiting compared to desktop alternatives. Specific missing features include duplicate-line shortcuts and copy-all-output functionality. The author notes that the fixed shortcuts are sometimes incompatible with browser and OS combinations, particularly when switching between macOS and Windows. Despite this criticism, the author praises Databricks as the best data engineering and ML platform available.

## Key claims
- Databricks notebooks do not support keyboard shortcut customization.
- The author has approximately 20 years of experience with JetBrains IDEs such as IntelliJ and PyCharm.
- Missing shortcut features include duplicate line and copy all output commands.
- Fixed shortcuts in Databricks notebooks can conflict with browser and OS-level shortcuts on macOS and Windows.
- The author worked directly with AmpLab contributors in 2014 and considers themselves a long-time Databricks user.
- The author describes Databricks as the best data engineering and ML platform available, built around Apache Spark.
