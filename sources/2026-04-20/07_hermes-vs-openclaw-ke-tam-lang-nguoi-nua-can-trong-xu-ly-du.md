## Summary
The article compares Hermes and OpenCLAW, two different approaches to data processing in the era of Big Data and AI. Hermes is suitable for projects that prioritize orchestration and data flow, while OpenCLAW is ideal for projects that require intense computational power. The article provides a brief overview of each approach, their strengths, and applications. It also offers advice on how to choose between the two, suggesting that combining both may be the best approach for large projects.

## Key claims
- Hermes is based on a directed acyclic graph (DAG) model and is suitable for projects that require orchestration and data flow.
- OpenCLAW is optimized for heterogeneous computing and is ideal for projects that require intense computational power.
- Hermes is easy to use and can be scaled out on Kubernetes, while OpenCLAW is more challenging to use and requires expertise in C, CUDA, or other low-level programming languages.
- Combining both Hermes and OpenCLAW may be the best approach for large projects that require both orchestration and intense computational power.
- Hermes can be used for building data lakehouses, pipelines for e-commerce, and CRM synchronization, while OpenCLAW can be used for training large language models, genetic analysis, and complex physics simulations.
