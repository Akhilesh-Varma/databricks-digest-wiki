## Summary
OxyEcomBench is a new multimodal benchmark designed to evaluate large language models and multimodal large language models on real-world e-commerce tasks. It comprises approximately 6,300 high-quality bilingual Chinese–English instances spanning 29 tasks across 6 capability aspects, covering platform operators, merchants, and customers. The benchmark uses a difficulty-aware four-level rubric (P0–P3) and prioritizes visually salient multimodal cases where key evidence resides in images. Evaluations of 20 mainstream LLMs and MLLMs reveal that even leading models achieve only modest performance, highlighting a significant gap between general-purpose and e-commerce-specific capabilities. The authors attribute this gap to insufficient e-commerce domain knowledge infused during model training.

## Key claims
- OxyEcomBench contains approximately 6,300 high-quality instances for bilingual Chinese–English e-commerce evaluation.
- The benchmark covers 29 tasks across 6 capability aspects, addressing platform operators, merchants, and customers.
- A difficulty-aware four-level rubric (P0–P3) is applied to all 29 tasks where stable expert consensus on difficulty exists.
- All benchmark data is sourced from authentic e-commerce platforms and verified by domain experts.
- Evaluations of 20 mainstream LLMs and MLLMs show that even leading models attain only modest performance on OxyEcomBench.
- Performance gaps between models narrow on OxyEcomBench, suggesting that insufficient e-commerce-specific knowledge mutes the advantages of advanced general-purpose models.
- Existing e-commerce benchmarks typically adopt a single stakeholder perspective or target a narrow set of tasks, limiting holistic assessment.
