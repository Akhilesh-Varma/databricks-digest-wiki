## Summary
The 2026 State of Data Engineering Survey, based on 1,101 respondents, reveals that data engineers spend roughly 60% of their time on reactive work: 34% on data quality and reliability and 26% on firefighting. Despite this, only 10.1% of respondents cite data quality as their biggest bottleneck, suggesting teams have normalized the problem. A key finding is that ad-hoc data modeling correlates with 38% of time spent firefighting, while teams using canonical or semantic models spend only 19%. The survey identifies lack of ownership, pressure to move fast, and poor requirements as the structural drivers of this reactive cycle. Recommended mitigations include assigning data quality ownership, investing in canonical data modeling, and automating anomaly detection.

## Key claims
- Data engineers spend 60% of their time reacting to problems: 34% on data quality/reliability and 26% on firefighting.
- Only 10.1% of survey respondents cited data quality as their biggest bottleneck, despite it consuming the most time.
- Ad-hoc data modeling (used by 17.4% of respondents) correlates with 38% of time spent firefighting, compared to 19% for teams using canonical or semantic models.
- 59.3% of respondents cited pressure to move fast as their top data modeling pain point.
- 50.7% of respondents cited lack of clear ownership as a top modeling pain point.
- 82% of data professionals use AI tools daily, with code generation (82%) and documentation (56%) dominating use cases.
- 43.8% of respondents run on cloud data warehouses and 26.8% on lakehouses.
