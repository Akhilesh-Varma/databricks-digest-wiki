## Summary
This Databricks community roundup highlights five technical blog posts covering enterprise data topics. The posts address FinOps architecture for cross-account cost visibility, query performance tuning for Databricks Serverless SQL, safe data experimentation best practices, a metadata-driven ETL framework, and expanded Apache Iceberg support in Unity Catalog. Notably, Databricks is expanding Iceberg support in Unity Catalog with general availability for Managed Iceberg, Iceberg v3, and Foreign Iceberg, along with new governance and federation capabilities.

## Key claims
- Large organizations often operate multiple Databricks workspaces across cloud accounts, regions, and business units, requiring repeatable FinOps architecture for cross-account cost visibility.
- Databricks Serverless SQL (DBSQL) is Databricks' latest offering for building data warehouses on the Lakehouse.
- Databricks is expanding Apache Iceberg support in Unity Catalog with GA releases for Managed Iceberg, Iceberg v3, and Foreign Iceberg.
- New Unity Catalog capabilities include open sharing, federation, and governance across multiple engines.
- A metadata-driven ETL framework in Databricks is presented as a solution for complex data flows in modern data-driven enterprises.
- Best practices for safe data experimentation are aimed at developers and data scientists building models, testing hypotheses, and constructing data pipelines.
