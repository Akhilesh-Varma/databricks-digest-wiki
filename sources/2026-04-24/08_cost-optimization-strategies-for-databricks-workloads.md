## Summary
Databricks is a powerful platform for data engineering, analytics, and machine learning, but it can become expensive to operate if not managed carefully. To optimize costs, it's essential to understand where costs come from, including compute usage, storage costs, data transfer, and inefficient queries. By making smarter architectural and operational decisions, teams can reduce costs while maintaining performance and reliability. This guide explores practical strategies for cost optimization in Databricks, including optimizing cluster usage, improving query efficiency, and optimizing data storage. By implementing these strategies, teams can reduce their cloud bills and make the most of their Databricks investment.

## Key claims
- Databricks can become expensive to operate if not managed carefully
- Understanding where costs come from is essential for cost optimization
- Optimizing cluster usage can help reduce costs
- Improving query efficiency can reduce compute usage and costs
- Optimizing data storage can reduce storage costs and improve performance
