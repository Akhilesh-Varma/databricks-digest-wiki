## Summary
PepsiCo, one of the world's largest food and beverage companies, is modernizing a 27-year-old fragmented business intelligence landscape by consolidating onto a single governed analytics and AI platform built on Azure Databricks SQL and Unity Catalog. The migration has delivered an 80% cost reduction on a major sales analytics workload, dropping expenses from approximately $500K to $175K. Core financial and commercial tables are now built once in Databricks SQL and served to Power BI and Tableau, improving processing times by around 50%. The initiative also unifies data from multiple legacy warehouses and sector data lakes into a single enterprise data foundation, enabling the retirement of Synapse and Teradata systems while preparing for AI-driven analytics at scale.

## Key claims
- PepsiCo reduced costs on a major sales analytics workload by 80%, from approximately $500K to $175K, by shifting to DBSQL serverless.
- Core financial and commercial tables built in Databricks SQL improved processing times by around 50% for Power BI and Tableau consumers.
- PepsiCo is consolidating data from multiple legacy warehouses and sector data lakes into a single enterprise data foundation on Azure Databricks.
- The platform migration provides a repeatable path to retire legacy systems including Synapse and Teradata.
- Unity Catalog is used to provide governance across PepsiCo's unified analytics and AI platform.
- PepsiCo's architecture uses a serverless infrastructure to perform AI analytics at scale efficiently.
