## Summary
The article argues that well-engineered data platforms are insufficient without dedicated product management for data products. It contends that data products require a product manager to ensure they deliver real business value rather than just technical correctness. The piece explores what the product manager role means in the context of data products, distinguishing it from traditional engineering or analytics roles. It emphasizes that treating data as a product requires ownership, stakeholder alignment, and iterative improvement similar to software product management.

## Key claims
- Having a technically excellent data platform is not enough to ensure data products deliver business value.
- Data products require dedicated product management to succeed.
- A data product manager is responsible for stakeholder alignment and ensuring data assets meet real business needs.
- Treating data as a product means applying iterative improvement and ownership principles from software product management.
- Even well-modeled lakehouses and flawless pipelines can fail without proper product ownership.
